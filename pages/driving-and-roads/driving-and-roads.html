Driving and Roads

Information about road conditions, driver's licences, vehicle registration, Drive Clean, and commercial vehicles.

Driver licensing

Licensed drivers

  • Renew a licence that will expire soon or a licence that expired less than 1 year ago
  • Pay defaulted fines and driver’s licence reinstatement
  • Renew a licence that expired more than 1 year ago
  • Renew your licence, if you are 80 years or older
  • Change the personal information (name, address, etc.) that appears on your licence
  • Replace a lost, stolen or damaged licence
  • Get an accessible parking permit or find out how to renew or replace a permit

Visitors, travellers, new residents

  • Visiting Ontario: know what you need to drive here
  • Travelling outside Ontario: renew your licence while you’re away
  • Crossing U.S. border: if you’re going by road or water, get an enhanced driver’s licence to use instead of a passport
  • Moving to Ontario: switch your licence for an Ontario driver’s licence

New drivers

  • Get a G driver’s licence
    (car, van or small truck)
  • Get a motorcycle licence
  • Book or cancel a road test
  • Driver's handbook
  • Driving schools

Truck and bus drivers

  • Get a truck driver’s licence
    (full Class A, restricted Class A and Class)
  • Get a bus driver’s licence
    (school bus, transit and ambulance)

Penalized drivers

  • Learn how demerit points work
  • Reinstate a suspended licence
  • Order a driver record
    (uncertified and certified by Ontario)

Vehicle registration

  • Buy or sell a used vehicle
  • Register and insure your vehicle
  • Order a personalized licence plate
  • Replace a lost, stolen, or damaged licence plate

Renew a licence plate sticker

Renew your licence plate sticker online up to six months before it expires until 11:59 p.m. on the expiry date.

Drive Clean

Older vehicles need to pass an emissions test before you can renew their registration and licence plates.
