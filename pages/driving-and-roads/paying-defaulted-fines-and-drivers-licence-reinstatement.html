Paying defaulted fines and driver's license reinstatement

Defaulted driver fines must be paid in full before you can renew your driver’s licence, order a licence plate sticker or order a personalized licence plate.

Before you pay

Defaulted driver fines are for any unpaid moving violation and do not include vehicle (parking or highway toll) fines.

Examples of moving violations are:

  • speeding
  • drinking and driving
  • careless driving
  • racing

Learn more about demerit points and infractions

You may have to pay a reinstatement fee/administrative monetary penalty (AMP) if your licence was suspended.

Your licence could be suspended:

  • at the side of the road (a roadside suspension)
  • a court could order a suspension
  • if you receive a suspension letter from the Ministry of Transportation

Learn more about licence suspension

Pay Online

Default fines and/or reinstatement

To pay defaulted fines and reinstatement fees online you need:

  • your driver’s licence number
  • the suspension number (located on the “Notice of Suspension of Driver’s Licence” document sent by the Ministry of Transportation)

    • or

  • the offence number (located on the “Notice of Fine and Due Date” or the “Fine Order” form from the courts)

Reinstatement/ Administrative Monetary Penalty (AMP) only

To pay only the reinstatement fee/Administrative Monetary Penalty (AMP) fee online you need:

  • your driver’s licence number
  • the suspension number (located on the “Notice of Suspension of Driver’s Licence” document sent by the Ministry of Transportation)

To pay defaulted fines and reinstatement fees in-person you need:

  • your driver’s licence number

Visit a ServiceOntario centre

Driving eligibility

The payment of the driver’s licence fines and/or reinstatement fee does not guarantee that you will be eligible to drive.

If you are eligible to drive:

  • a “Notice of Outstanding Licensing Requirements” including a temporary driver’s licence will be mailed to you the next business day.
    • a temporary driver’s licence can be obtained immediately at any ServiceOntario centre if needed.
  • a new driver’s licence will be mailed to you in 4-5 weeks.

Driver's licence number or suspension number

If you don’t know your driver’s licence number or the suspension number, contact ServiceOntario at:

Toll-free in Canada: 1-800-387-3445

Toronto (GTA): 416-235-2999

Toll-free TTY in Canada: 1-800-268-7095

Toronto (GTA) TTY: 416-325-3408

Court information:

If you don’t have the “Notice of Fine and Due Date”, or the “Fine Order” form, contact the court(s) where you received the fine(s).

  • Criminal Courts
  • Provincial offnces courts

Address changed?

If you have recently moved, by law you must change your address within 6 days of moving.

If your address has changed and you have not notified us, you can update your address online with ServiceOntario.
