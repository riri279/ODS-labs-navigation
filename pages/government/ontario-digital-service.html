About Us: Simper, better, faster services

Across Ontario, people access government services and information online, everyday.

It’s time we made it easier to do that – whether it’s ordering a vehicle permit, applying for student aid or finding a health service near home.

Enter Digital Government. It’s our commitment to improve the online experience for citizens and transform the most important government services.

How We'll Do It

A better user experience

Re-design a series of high-impact signature projects to visibly improve the citizen’s experience with government.

Top digital talent

Attract, nurture and empower the next generation of digital talent through creating new work opportunities.

Consistently good services

Establish a new, modern set of common digital service standards for the organization.

A dedicated digital office

Hillary Hartley has been appointed as our first Chief Digital Officer to lead a new digital service team and transformation across government.

Open, participatory government

Make it easier for people to participate in, and provide input on, government decisions.

An inclusive, digitally enabled province

Create opportunities for people to advance or gain new digital skills, especially those most in need.

Milestones

APRIL 2017

Code for Canada

Ontario has partnered with tech leaders to co-found Code for Canada, a not-for-profit organization that develops digital solutions to make government services more user-friendly and easier to access. Code for Canada connects coders and designers with governments to enhance digital literacy and develop technology-based solutions to improve peoples' lives.

Winter/spring 2017

The Ontario Government @ The Communitech Hub

An Ontario digital outpost will open among other tech and non-tech organizations using 50,000+ square feet dedicated to world-leading innovation and collaboration in Kitchener-Waterloo.

January 2017

Ontario Internship Program – Digital ( I+IT Stream)

A new stream of the Ontario Internship Program will be introduced to recruit in-demand digital skills to the organization, providing high-impact opportunities to recent graduates looking to use their talents and passion to improve online services.

March 2018

Digital Government Action Plan

Ontario has released an action plan that will serve as the public roadmap for Ontario’s digital transformation, focusing on enabling better online services designed around user needs and equipping the province to lead in the digital era.
